Projects

Covid-19 Detection Using Federated Learning

In our study, we implemented a pseudo-distributed training method for federated learning, which was carried out on a single machine. Each participant involved in the experiment had their own individual model, which was reset to the centrally updated model after each central model update. All these models were developed using Tensorflow, and the training and test images were resized to either (224, 224) or (299, 299) dimensions for model training. The terminal participants employed the Adam optimizer with a learning rate of 0.001. Our research aims to propose the application of federated learning as an approach to train COVID-19 data while effectively addressing privacy concerns. Through the use of federated learning, we conducted experiments to assess its accuracy in diagnosing COVID-19. Furthermore, our goal was to compare the performance of three well-known models, namely VGG19, MobileNet, and InceptionV3, both within and outside the federated learning framework. We utilized an X-ray dataset with four participants, and the federated learning framework was trained using the Free Google Colab Plan.
